Practice Areas

Business Formation & Corporate Law:
We help entrepreneurs choose the right business structure (LLC, Corporation, Partnership, Sole Proprietorship). Our team handles articles of incorporation, operating agreements, shareholder agreements, and business licenses. Typical timeline for business formation is 2-4 weeks depending on the state.

Employment Law:
We represent both employers and employees in workplace disputes. Services include drafting employment contracts, non-compete agreements, severance packages, workplace harassment claims, wrongful termination cases, and wage disputes. We recommend all businesses with 5+ employees have an employee handbook reviewed annually.

Intellectual Property:
Our IP practice covers trademark registration, copyright protection, trade secret agreements, and patent consultation. Trademark registration typically takes 8-12 months through the USPTO. We advise all businesses to conduct a trademark search before investing in branding.

Contract Law:
We draft, review, and negotiate all types of business contracts including vendor agreements, service contracts, lease agreements, licensing deals, and partnership agreements. We recommend having any contract over $10,000 in value reviewed by an attorney.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to form an LLC?
A: Typically 2-4 weeks. We handle all paperwork, file with the state, obtain your EIN, and draft your operating agreement.

Q: Do I need a trademark for my business name?
A: If you plan to operate beyond your local area or want to prevent others from using your name, yes. A federal trademark gives you nationwide protection.

Q: What should I do if I receive a cease and desist letter?
A: Don't ignore it. Contact us immediately for a free assessment. We'll review the claims, advise on validity, and craft an appropriate response.

Q: How often should I update my employee handbook?
A: At minimum annually, or whenever employment laws change in your state. We offer an annual review service for $500.

Q: Can I use a template contract I found online?
A: We strongly advise against it for anything significant. Online templates often lack jurisdiction-specific language, may be outdated, and rarely account for your specific business needs.
